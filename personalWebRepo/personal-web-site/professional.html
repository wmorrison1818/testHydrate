Google Research and Machine Intelligence Software Engineering Intern

Unfortunately much of my work at Google was confidential, however, I can say that I worked to develop an internal computer vision API, which will likely be used in Google products in the future. DST Systems Architect Intern

During the summer between my sophomore and freshman years of college I was a systems architect intern at DST Systems. DST is a fairly large company in Kansas City, which handles the movement of important information like mutual fund transactions or pharmaceutical information movement. The internship was paid and DST paid for my housing for the summer. As an intern I worked on DST’s flagship software AWD, which helps bureaucracies function within a business. I developed interfaces for inputting client information into several different databases. I also helped DST integrate Splunk into their systems. Splunk is a data-mining program for searching through error logs. When I began my internship, Splunk was new to DST. The engineers wanted to use Splunk to detect why their systems were failing and predict the next failures. I used Splunk to index DST’s 100s of millions of error logs to make the error logs more accessible to the engineers. Because Splunk was so new to DST I was one of the most influential engineers in its integration. Indexing AWD’s error logs also familiarized me with the workings of a complex system. I was the only intern with my team, so I was largely treated like a full time employee. I attended major product development meetings and I learned how businesses function.
